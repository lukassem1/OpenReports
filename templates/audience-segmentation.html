In summary · What to do with each segment

Separate segments, separate campaigns

Because the groups do not overlap, each one gets its own campaign path.

The [Segment A] segment is the lighter band: 800 contacts in the lower band, 230 of them also meeting [qualifier X] — where a low-cost [campaign A] is most likely to pay off. The [Segment B] segment is the priority queue: 3,000 contacts in the upper band, with 720 meeting [qualifier X] and almost the entire population (710 [touchpoint metric P] and 40.5k [reach metric Q]). And there are 2,850 upper-band contacts not meeting [qualifier X], which call for re-scoping. Treating each segment at its own pace captures value and keeps the base clean — without mixing populations.
