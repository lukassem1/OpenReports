Positioning & experimentation

The flag, and what to test — in what order

Every piece carries a number or a case alongside the thesis. Provocation without proof becomes the very hype you're fighting.

Public flag (point of view)

“[A sharp five-word point of view.]”

Memorable, debatable, hits the ROI pain. Short, repeatable by others.

Institutional · C-level

“[The calm, defensible version.]”

You don't need to code or memorise prompts — you need to learn to solve problems. Sober, defensible, never burns a bridge.

Data-anchored

“[N]% tried. Only [N]% see returns.”

The problem was never the tool. Lends calm authority and shields against “you're just being contrarian.”

What is NOT an A/B — clearing the confusion. “Current state vs. future state” is not a test: it's the backbone of the method (you don't test whether to breathe; you breathe). It's the market's biggest gap, the engine of the anchor case, and the hook that converts best. It's always on. The real A/Bs are about marketing — how you sell access to the method, not the method itself.

Sequential pilots, not parallel A/B

With dozens of leads a month, splitting traffic only produces noise read as signal — worse than not testing. Statistical A/B needs ~1,000 visitors or ~30–50 conversions per variant (realistic only around month 6–9). Until then: one bet at a time, 2–4 weeks each, decided on strong qualitative signal. Lock one variable before testing the next.

Risks to harden against

SEVERE

“It's a terminal, but you don't code”

Sounds contradictory to a beginner. Mitigation: hardened onboarding + “first win in 20 minutes” in Week 2. What you teach is steering an agent — proven in the first session, not asserted.

SEVERE

Data privacy / security

A beginner hands real data to an external agent without a legal basis. Mitigation: turn the objection into a module and a differentiator. Checklist embedded in the flow.

SEVERE

Vendor dependency

Pricing/policy outside your control. Mitigation: decouple method from tool conceptually — the method is “diagnose + ask right + validate”; the current implementation is [tool]. If the tool changes, the method survives.

SEVERE

Revenue capped by the founder

1:1 is the differentiator and the cage. Mitigation: package in layers — 1:1 becomes the premium tier; below it, community + cohort; above it, train-the-trainer. The method scales; the founder doesn't.

MODERATE

Finite first-mover window (12–18 months)

Mitigation: build the brand moat now — a named methodology + a body of measured cases + authority on your channel. The moat isn't being first; it's being the name when the market matures.

MODERATE

“Expensive for the segment” without social proof

When raising to $4,997. Mitigation: the founder price buys the cases; only raise after 5 solid ones.
